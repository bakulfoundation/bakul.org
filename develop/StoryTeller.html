JUST THE BEST

The Lineup Artists-Headliners

Bongiswa Kotta- Ramushwana

The most famous storyteller from Africa. She started her career in Storytelling in 2004 in Johannesburg, South Africa. Her passion is driven by the love of telling stories and she enjoys the company of her audience especially children. She speaks eight South African Languages and the Sign Language. She is involved in organizing the Ungasali International Storytelling Festival and facilitates workshops nationally and internationally. Currently Bongiswa is working at Freedom Park Heritage Site in Pretoria as a professional Storyteller.

Ariyo Zidni

Ariyo Zidni, also known as "Kak Aio", is a well-known storyteller of Indonesia. He is a storyteller with unique voice and facial mannerism. He is the founder of Ayo Dongeng Indonesia (Storytelling Community) and Indonesia International Storytelling Festival. Ariyo was drawn to the social healing aspect of storytelling since telling stories at hospitals in 1999, and now he is a childrenâ€™s story writer, storytelling coach, and lecturer on Children Literature Subject. He has been invited to tell in various international storytelling festivals in Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam, South Korea, India, America, and many others.

Nupur Aggarwal

The Festival Curator of BhuFeSto 2023 is Nupur Aggarwal, a well known storyteller and storytelling coach. A nutritionist by education and a storyteller by passion, Nupur provides nourishment and nutrition for the soul through a carefully chosen and healthy diet of stories. For the past 8 years, she has been a Story Trainer and Coach helping people leverage storytelling in their work. Her latest release "Storytelling Made Easy", is a resource book on storytelling for aspiring storytellers, educators, librarians, parents, children, and all those who work with children.

Roger Jenkins

Roger Jenkins is a famous Singaporean storyteller of British origin. He has won many coveted international awards for storytelling such as the Best Storyteller Award at the 16th Kanoon International Story Festival, Iran, 2013. He is a Founding Director of the leading association of Storytellers in Asia, FEAST (Federation of Asian Storytellers, 2018- present.). He founded HI! Theatre, Singapore's theatre of the deaf in 1986. He was awarded the "Friend of the Deaf" award in 1990. He also co-founded The Madhatters Comedy Company, a local professional improvisational comedy group. Jenkins conducts the very popular "Make Me A Storyteller" workshop in schools.

Sujit Mahapatra

The Festival Director of BhuFeSto 2023 is Sujit Mahapatra, the founder and volunteer of Bakul Foundation, a movement for volunteerism in Odisha, which has been trying to promote reading by setting up libraries, publishing children's books and organizing storytelling events. Sujit curated the popular web series, STORYTIME @ 9 and RABIBAR GAPABAAR, where he featured the best storytellers from 50 different countries on youtube.com/bakulfoundation . Sujit had conceptualized and was the Festival Director of BhuFeSto in 2018.

Antonio Rocha

Antonio Rocha is an award-winning internationally acclaimed storyteller. He is a native of Brazil, is based in Portland, USA. He studied mime with Master Marcel Marceau and his unique fusion of mime and spoken word has been featured at premier venues in the United States, and across the 6 continents. He has won the coveted Circle of Excellence Award by the National Storytelling Network in the US.

Marion Kenny

Marion Kenny is a charismatic live performer, highly regarded multi-instrumentalist, master storyteller and inspirational trainer living in Edinburgh, Scotland. She has been a performer with theatre, circus and physical theatre companies since 1985. She plays concert flute, Balinese bamboo flute (Suling), Chinese bamboo flute, Chinese reed flute (Bawu), Balinese bamboo flute (Suling), the Chinese harp (Guzheng) and piano.
